Scheduling Overview

Scheduling Flow Description

This is the beginning basics for building classes for your school. It is important to understand the flow of each step described.

The following items are “Yearly” file items (there is a school year associated with each). The next school year is created approximately the first of November. Any changes made to these files in the current year after November 1st will not be copied to the next school year.


  • 1) District Course Master - The District Course Master is maintained by Student Services. It contains information at the district level about ALL courses that are available to the schools. It is important to understand the set up in the District Course Master and how it affects each course. The District Course Master is broken down by school type and defines for each course number the allowable grading type, graduation requirements, special ed, honors, etc.

    For all secondary schools course number range 25000 – 25999 allows for additional access for teachers assigned to a course in this range. It provides access for the teacher to see ALL progress reports for assigned students. Used for advisory, options, labs, etc. that teachers need to track students progress.

  • 2) Course Definitions - Course Definitions contain information at the school level about ALL courses taught at your school. The course must be defined for your school before it can be added to your master schedule. Course Definitions are maintained by the school registrar. It defines for each course number the allowable grading type, graduation requirements, credits, etc.

  • 3) Teachers - Each teacher must have a defined Teacher ID for your school before you can assign a class to them. The school Teacher ID is linked to the teachers employee number in the employee master file. The link between the employee number and courses is what generates class rosters to the grade books and gives teachers their MyStudent access.

  • 4) Master Schedule - The master schedule is what defines every course/section (classroom group) being taught at your school. Each course/section combination has an unique identifying number and allows designation of teacher, class size, room number, etc.
